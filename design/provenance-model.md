---

## Why this exists

Perspectives should feel authored and accountable without claiming a publishing workflow that is not yet real. The launch site presents essays and field notes as Volant Labs thinking. Rich graph-drafted provenance can return later, once the workflow exists and is worth explaining publicly.

## Public labels for launch

| Kind | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Essay | Human-authored piece with a named byline. |
| Field note | Edited Volant Labs working note, launch note, or observation. |
| Artifact *(later)* | A produced document, diagram, or decision record once that workflow is public-ready. |

Do not use "From the graph," "Drafted from the graph," "Ratified," or "Ratified by" in launch-facing labels.

## What Shows Where

**Library card (Perspectives):**

1. Kind pill: Essay / Field note.
2. One accountability line: "By Eddie Austin" or "Volant Labs field note."

**Article page:**

The side panel is **Editorial context**, not a formal provenance claim. It can include:

- Source
- Editorial layer
- Owner
- Status
- Open question
- Counterpoint
- What would change this

This keeps the useful epistemic discipline without making graph-drafted publication sound like a mature product feature.

## Filters

Perspectives filters by public kind only: All · Essays · Field notes.

## Future State

The richer graph provenance model can come back when it is operationally true: graph-drafted content, named human review, explicit status, and clear source evidence. Until then, public copy should stay plain.
