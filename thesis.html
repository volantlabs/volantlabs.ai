Foundation story

The graph remembers. The model reasons. The human ratifies.

Vellis rests on a simple thesis: agentic systems need more than prompts and documents. They need a substrate where meaning persists, policy executes, and human judgment compounds.

01 / The arc

Capability is abundant. Coherence is scarce.

Vellis starts from the failure mode that shows up once teams add more models, tools, agents, and automations: everyone can move faster, but the system can stop agreeing with itself.

01

Speed without memory becomes drift.

Documents, chats, tickets, and dashboards are useful, but they rarely preserve the operational meaning behind a decision.

Pressure: local context decays.
02

Text alone cannot carry the system.

Agents can read text, but execution needs typed things: who can change what, which link means dependency, what counts as approved.

Need: executable semantics.
03

The graph is a theory about the work.

A graph is not just storage. It is a living model of what the organization believes is true enough to act on.

Move: make meaning queryable.
04

Governance belongs where execution happens.

Policies, traces, approvals, and provenance matter most at write time, when agents and humans are changing shared reality.

Result: speed and trust together.

02 / The operating loop

Meaning should compound every time the system runs.

Each cycle should make the next one clearer. Vellis remembers structure, models reason over it, humans ratify the important moves, and the graph becomes a stronger substrate for the next action.

01Remember

Store typed nodes, links, provenance, and decisions.

02Reason

Let agents traverse meaning instead of guessing from text.

03Ratify

Keep human judgment visible where it changes the graph.

04Run

Execute work with policy near the write path.

05Compound

Turn each pass into better context for the next one.

03 / The promise

Open substrate. Governed graduation.

One property stays clear: Vellis is real, useful, and yours to run. Kesher is the calm next layer when audit, multi-tenant operations, and approval policy matter.

What stays open

The graph, schema, local runtime, and export path remain the heart of Vellis.

No account required to start.
Full-fidelity export stays part of the trust contract.
Domain explorations teach by showing real structures.

What graduates

Kesher answers the operational question Vellis should not pretend to solve alone.

Which agent changed this record?
Under which policy and approval path?
How does the organization prove the answer later?
