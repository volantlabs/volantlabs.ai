No rug-pull means more than a license

A ratified note on trust signals: portability, export path, and a commercial platform that does not compete with Vellis.

April 29, 2026 3 min read Drafted from the graph · Ratified by Eddie Austin
Ratified

Every Perspectives piece keeps authorship and ratification visible. The full provenance footer lives with the article.

TrustLicenseExport

A license is only the beginning

A permissive license matters, but it is not the whole trust signal. Users also need to know whether the project remains runnable, whether their data remains portable, and whether the commercial path will quietly make the open path irrelevant.

No rug-pull is a product promise as much as a legal one. It has to show up in the architecture.

The exit path has to be real

Full-fidelity export is the operational version of trust. It tells a team that the work they put into modeling their domain is not trapped inside a vendor's future pricing decision.

That promise is strongest when it is boring and visible: open formats, clear docs, and no mysterious downgrade between what Vellis stores and what the user can take with them.

The platform should graduate, not replace

Kesher has a real job: write-back gates, audit traces, approvals, and operational controls. Those capabilities can justify a commercial relationship without undermining Vellis.

That is the clean bargain. Vellis stays runnable. Kesher adds governance when an organization needs it. Nobody has to pretend that trust was solved by a license file alone.
